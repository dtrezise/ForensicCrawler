# Roadmap

Status: WORKING

## Phase 0 - Source reconciliation and governance

Deliverables:

- source-provenance review and CivicLedger exclusion;
- system, vision, requirements, standards, rights, security, threat, source-registry, confidence, and expert-pipeline documents;
- decision and editorial review logs; and
- safe pilot selection.

Exit: repository artifacts are source-labeled, cross-linked, and security-reviewed. No network activity is required.

## Phase 1 - Provenance-first ingestion MVP

Current local vertical slice:

- versioned deterministic package and source registry;
- normalized records and permanent IDs;
- metadata-only NASA pilot;
- metadata-only Charlie Kirk public-record case fixture;
- metadata-only Renee Good public-record case fixture;
- metadata-only Operation Southern Spear vessel-strike campaign fixture;
- claim/source/asset relationships and exact locators;
- timeline discrepancy and alternate-explanation view;
- rights, provenance, correction, and export inspection;
- deterministic import/export, report, audit, and link-policy tools;
- accessible responsive interface with automated tests;
- interactive schematic 3D scenes with explicit nonmetric and non-photogrammetric limits;
- case-specific procedural posture, time-zone display, dignity boundaries, and reconstruction-layer semantics;
- schema-v2 acquisition, source-family, underlying-asset, confidence, transformation, revision, methodology, glossary, and integrity records;
- deterministic unified Markdown/PDF reports and locally signed/verified manifests;
- global search, cross-case comparison, presentation mode, temporal scene states, semantic labels, and direct artifact links; and
- a project-owned calibrated multi-view benchmark with point-cloud, mesh, Gaussian-splat interchange, validation metrics, and a recorded COLMAP readiness gate.

Next network-gated work:

- approved local mock crawler and SSRF/robots/rate harness;
- project-owned user agent and contact route;
- source-specific manifest approval; and
- one minimal live canary only after launch blockers pass.

## Phase 2 - Timeline and contradiction engine

- time-zone, clock, precision, drift, frame, and interval normalization;
- multi-source synchronization and independence analysis;
- competing claims and alternate hypotheses;
- source-change monitoring without automated status mutation;
- private expert research and consent workflow; and
- larger deterministic review packages.

## Phase 3 - 2D reconstruction

- evidence-linked maps, diagrams, camera frusta, lines of sight, and movement paths;
- coordinate reference systems and uncertainty envelopes;
- reproducible transformation records; and
- observed/inferred/disputed/interpolated visibility toggles.

## Phase 4 - 3D reconstruction

Current precursor: three project-authored relational Three.js scenes prove evidence-layer, contradiction, uncertainty, attributed-account, state-transition, semantic labeling, selection, and transformation UX without claiming metric reconstruction. A separate synthetic benchmark proves the calibrated geometry and artifact contracts without importing those capabilities into case evidence.

- reviewed photogrammetry, multi-view geometry, depth estimation, and sensor fusion;
- evidence-linked scene graph and camera solutions;
- calibration, error bounds, alternative reconstructions, and independent reproduction; and
- visual realism constrained by evidentiary state.

## Phase 5 - Mobile field companion

- project-owned secure capture identity;
- on-device hashing and signed upload manifests;
- camera, LiDAR, IMU, microphone, and location consent/rights review;
- offline custody queue and conflict handling; and
- remote wipe, deletion, and incident response.

## Phase 6 - Advanced delivery

- restricted collaboration and public-viewer separation;
- VR/AR exploration and documentary APIs;
- enterprise access, retention, legal hold, backups, and compliance evidence; and
- independently reviewed export procedures.

## Phase 7 - Validated professional use

- independent security assessment;
- qualified legal review;
- reproducibility and inter-reviewer studies;
- expert and affected-community evaluation;
- standards/laboratory validation where applicable; and
- only then consideration of admissibility-related claims.

No phase is complete because its document exists. Each exit requires evidence from the quality gates in `PRODUCT_REQUIREMENTS.md` and `docs/security/LAUNCH_BLOCKERS.md`.
