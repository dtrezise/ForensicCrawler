# Vision and Scope

Status: WORKING

## Vision

Make complex event research inspectable. A user should be able to select a claim, timestamp, asset, source, movement, camera, transformation, or reconstruction element and see exactly what supports it, what contradicts it, what was inferred, what remains unknown, and what rights limit its use.

## Product principles

- provenance before presentation;
- primary-source preference without publisher infallibility;
- permanent identity and append-only history;
- evidence state separate from confidence and procedural status;
- contrary evidence and alternate explanations by default;
- minimal lawful collection and link-only storage by default;
- automation for monitoring and review, never automatic publication;
- reconstruction as an inspectable model, never a substitute recording;
- restrained, accessible, non-sensational design; and
- portable records and provider-neutral infrastructure boundaries.

## Phase 1 scope

- trusted source registry with source-specific authorization, rights, robots, rate, and retention fields;
- deterministic package import/export and validation;
- investigations, events, claims, revisions, sources, assets, time anchors, relationships, contradictions, rights decisions, confidence, audit events, corrections, and reviews;
- evidence cards with exact locators;
- timeline and time-source uncertainty;
- contradiction and alternate-explanation review;
- provenance and transformation inspection;
- private rights/compliance review surface;
- human-readable report export; and
- automated schema, integrity, rights, reference, and interface tests.

## Explicitly deferred

- network-connected crawling and browser automation;
- automated source authentication or rights conclusions;
- public or multi-tenant service;
- 2D scene calculation beyond a static evidence-linked demonstration;
- photogrammetry, NeRF, depth estimation, sensor fusion, ballistics, face recognition, or identity matching;
- mobile camera, microphone, LiDAR, or IMU capture;
- VR/AR, documentary APIs, enterprise administration, and professional evidence packages; and
- any claim of validated forensic, legal, laboratory, or court use.

## Prohibited uses

- rumor amplification, doxxing, stalking, mass surveillance, or dossier building;
- private-message, paywall, account, leaked-credential, or access-control bypass;
- automatic attribution of guilt, motive, identity, affiliation, or sensitive traits;
- vulnerability exploitation, CAPTCHA solving, block evasion, or proxy rotation;
- covert expert or source outreach; and
- automated publication or irreversible high-impact decisions.

## Assumptions requiring validation

| Assumption | Validation needed |
|---|---|
| Authorized professionals need a unified evidence and reconstruction workspace | Structured interviews and workflow observation with consent |
| Source-specific governance can be operationally maintained | Pilot registry review, ownership, expiry, and change-audit exercise |
| Deterministic packages can preserve enough context across tools | Round-trip, clean-clone, and independent reproduction study |
| Timeline and contradiction surfaces improve review quality | Task-based usability study against conventional notes/spreadsheets |
| 2D/3D methods can expose rather than hide uncertainty | Expert protocol, error bounds, calibration, and reproducibility study |
| Rights decisions can be structured without false automation | Qualified legal review and ambiguous-case evaluation |
| Professional exports can become audit-ready | Independent evidence-handling and jurisdiction-specific review |
