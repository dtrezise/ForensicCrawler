# Rights, Privacy, and Compliance

Status: WORKING - NOT LEGAL ADVICE OR LEGAL APPROVAL

## Decision surface

Every source and asset receives a versioned decision covering:

- authorization and lawful access basis;
- source terms and technical restrictions;
- robots and rate policy when automated access is considered;
- copyright owner, license, public-domain basis, third-party credits, and uncertainty;
- permitted acquisition, storage, transformation, display, quotation, and export;
- privacy, publicity, biometric, precise-location, confidential-source, and sensitive-data exposure;
- public-record status and any access or redistribution restriction;
- defamation, false-light, or misidentification risk;
- retention, deletion, legal hold, correction, and takedown requirements;
- jurisdictions and unresolved legal questions; and
- reviewer, date, expiry, rationale, and supporting locator.

## Default handling

| Material | Default |
|---|---|
| Clearly identified U.S. federal government work | Record the specific basis and third-party caveats; preserve only what the approved purpose needs |
| Government or court record | Verify actual custodian, access status, sealing/redaction, local restrictions, and redistribution terms |
| Clearly compatible open license | Record version, attribution, notice, share-alike, derivative, and commercial-use conditions |
| Project-licensed material | Store and use only the authorized bytes, purpose, users, period, and territory |
| Ordinary copyrighted reporting | Link and metadata by default; restrained excerpt only with documented rationale |
| Fair-use candidate | Quarantine or link-only pending review; the label never grants automatic storage or redistribution |
| Unknown or disputed rights | Link-only or reject until clarified |
| Sensitive personal or confidential source data | Minimize, restrict, redact, or reject; never enter ordinary logs or public packages |

## Apollo 11 pilot review

The pilot uses metadata and locators for:

- NASA's `Apollo 11 Mission Report`, which records landing at mission elapsed time `102:45:39.9`; and
- NASA's `Record of Lunar Events`, which lists landing at `102:45:43` and `20:17:43` GMT.

The repository does not copy the NASA PDF, imagery, audio, video, logos, insignia, or corrected Lunar Surface Journal commentary. NASA explains that much agency content is generally not subject to U.S. copyright, but logos/identifiers and third-party or identifiable-person material can have separate restrictions. The fixture therefore remains metadata-first and avoids endorsement or promotional use.

## Privacy and sensitive-data limits

Do not collect private addresses, private phone numbers, family information, medical information, financial account data, biometric identifiers, precise routine location, private messages, account data, credentials, or unrelated personal data merely because it is discoverable.

Sensitive-data processing requires a stated necessity, less-invasive alternatives, authority, access policy, retention, deletion path, reviewer, and explicit approval. Facial recognition and biometric inference are deferred and require a separate impact assessment and legal review.

## Confidential sources and experts

- Store candidates separately from evidence records.
- A candidate is not an adviser, contributor, source, supporter, or endorser.
- Keep private contact routes, consent, interview notes, conflicts, and restrictions out of public or ordinary analyst packages.
- No automation or agent initiates outreach.
- Consent must specify attribution, quotation, recording, retention, review, correction, and withdrawal terms.

## Correction and takedown

The process must:

1. accept a private project-owned request;
2. verify requester identity and authority proportionately;
3. freeze affected export/publication activity;
4. preserve the original record and append the request;
5. investigate sources, rights, privacy, and contrary evidence;
6. record disposition, rationale, reviewer, date, and appeal path;
7. propagate correction, restriction, or deletion across derivatives and exports; and
8. retain a minimized accountability event without retaining deleted personal content unnecessarily.

## Legal hold

A legal hold is a documented exception to normal deletion, not an invisible override. Record authority, scope, custodian, date, expiry/review, affected records, access restrictions, and release. Restored backups must replay deletion and hold state before becoming accessible.
