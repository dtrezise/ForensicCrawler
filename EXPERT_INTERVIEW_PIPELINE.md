# Expert Interview Pipeline

Status: WORKING - PRIVATE RESEARCH MODEL ONLY

No candidate has been researched or contacted for the Apollo 11 fixture. This document defines the future private workflow.

## Investigation companion brief

For each investigation, maintain a separately permissioned brief with:

- permanent investigation ID;
- question and decision the expertise should test;
- relevant expert domains;
- at least two independently verified potential candidates per material domain when feasible;
- public professional-profile routes only;
- candidate-specific fit rationale;
- questions designed to challenge the working interpretation;
- conflicts, institutional ties, limits, and uncertainty;
- verification and review dates;
- outreach status; and
- consent, attribution, recording, quotation, retention, correction, and withdrawal status.

## Candidate states

- research lead;
- identity/professional route verified;
- conflicts under review;
- proposed for approval;
- outreach approved;
- contacted;
- declined;
- consented participant;
- interviewed;
- review/quotation pending;
- closed or withdrawn.

A candidate is never described as a contributor, adviser, source, supporter, or endorser until the relevant consent and participation state supports that description.

## Separation and privacy

- Candidate records are not evidence records.
- Private contact information and outreach notes never enter ordinary exports or public builds.
- Public professional routes do not imply consent to contact, name, quote, or publish.
- Access is limited to the sourcing/consent workflow and audited.
- Retention is purpose-limited; declined or unused private records are deleted on schedule.

## Approval gate

No automation or agent may send a message, schedule a meeting, submit a form, or initiate outreach. DAN NEEDED before any contact. The approval must name the candidate, purpose, channel, message, attribution intent, and data-handling plan.

## Interview integrity

- State the project, purpose, recording method, intended use, and correction process.
- Ask for contrary evidence and alternative explanations.
- Separate recollection, direct observation, professional inference, hearsay, and speculation.
- Return consequential quotations or technical interpretations for accuracy review without granting editorial veto unless agreed.
- Preserve the original interview record, transcript version, corrections, consent changes, and withdrawal history under access controls.
